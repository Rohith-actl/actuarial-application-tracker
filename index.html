How to Use This Tracker
📋 What is this?
This is a curated application tracker for actuarial students across Australia. It lists companies across every major sector that hire actuarial graduates and interns in Australia, with programme details, dates, and notes. You can track where you've applied, what stage you're at, and filter down to companies most relevant to you.
Tip: Your application statuses and any companies you add are saved in your browser: they'll still be there next time you open the page.
📊 Column Guide
Company
The employer name. Click the ↗ link icon to go directly to their careers page. Click anywhere else on the row to expand it and see full notes, details, and the direct careers link. Companies you add yourself will show a "custom" badge.
Dates
When applications are typically open. Most grad and intern programmes open in Feb–Mar for the following year. Email Based means the company doesn't advertise publicly: you'll need to reach out directly or through your network.
Programme Type
The type of actuarial work the role involves: e.g. Pricing / Reserving, Actuarial Consulting, Risk / Analytics. This helps you understand what day-to-day work looks like before applying.
Student Interest
A relative measure of how many actuarial students typically apply to this company. Very High means it's extremely competitive (e.g. Big 4, major banks). Low means fewer students apply: often because the company is less well-known, smaller, or more specialised: which can mean less competition.
Intake
An estimate of how many graduates or interns are hired each cycle. Click the column header to sort by intake size.
Small (1–5 hires) Medium (5–15 hires) Large (15+ hires)
Type
Grad = graduate programme (for final-year students). Intern = internship only (usually penultimate year). Grad + Intern = the company offers both. A dash (-) means the programme type isn't confirmed.
Pre-penu Badge
A gold "Pre-penu" badge means the company has a specific programme open to pre-penultimate students (students in their second-to-last year or earlier). The programme name is shown when you expand the row. If there's no badge, apply in your penultimate or final year.
Status
Your personal tracking column. Update this as you progress through each company's hiring process. Changes save automatically.
Applied Online Assessment Interview Offer
Notes
Curated context about the company's actuarial programme: things like rotation structure, team size, technical focus, and international opportunities. Expand a row to read the full note.
✏️ Editing Details
You can personalise the details of any company directly in the table: not just your own additions, but the built-in ones too. Hover over a cell in the Dates, Programme Type, Student Interest, Intake, or Type columns and you'll see a pencil icon (✎) appear. Click it to edit.
Dates
Free-text input: type anything (e.g. Mar – May). Press Enter to save or Escape to cancel.
Programme Type
Free-text input: update to reflect the specific role you're targeting. Press Enter to save.
Student Interest
Dropdown selector: choose from Very High, High, Medium, Low–Med, or Low based on your own observations.
Intake
Interactive dot picker: click the dots to set Small (1 dot), Medium (2 dots), or Large (3 dots). Click the single active dot to clear it back to unknown. Changes apply instantly.
Type
Dropdown selector: choose Grad, Intern, or Grad + Intern.
All edits are saved automatically in your browser and persist across sessions. A small blue dot (●) appears next to any field you've changed from its original value, so you can see at a glance what you've personalised. To undo all edits at once, use the Reset All button.
🔍 Filters & Search
Search bar
Searches across company name, programme type, sector, and notes simultaneously. Useful for finding companies by keyword, e.g. "pricing", "reinsurance", or "international".
Sector buttons
Filter by industry sector. General = general insurance (car, home, commercial). Re = reinsurance (insurance for insurers). Life = life insurance and income protection. Health = private health insurers. Super = superannuation funds. Banking = banks and financial institutions. Consulting = Big 4 and actuarial consulting firms. Investment = asset managers and hedge funds. Data = data analytics companies. Government = government-run insurance schemes.
Status filter
Show only companies at a particular application stage. Useful for reviewing all your active applications or checking what's still Not Started.
Interest filter
Filter by student interest level. If you want less competition, try filtering for Medium or Low–Med: these companies are often just as good but receive fewer applications.
Type filter
Filter by Grad (graduate roles), Intern (internship roles), or Pre-penultimate (programmes specifically open to students in their second-to-last year or earlier).
Column sorting
Click any column header (Company, Dates, Programme Type, Student Interest, Intake) to sort the table. Click again to reverse the order. Use "✕ clear sort" to return to the default sector grouping.
Resize & collapse
columns
Hover over the border between any two column headers to see the resize handle. Drag it left or right to resize the column. Click it to collapse the column down to a thin line, useful for hiding columns you don't need (e.g. Notes or Type). Click the same line again to restore it. Use "Reset columns" in the results bar to restore all column widths to their defaults.
📈 Header Progress Bar
The top bar shows a real-time summary of your progress across all companies: how many you've applied to, and how many are at OA, Interview, and Offer stage. The progress bar fills as you mark companies with any status.
➕ Adding a Company
Click "+ Add Company" in the top right to add any company not already on the list. Only the company name and sector are required: all other fields (dates, programme type, interest level, intake, careers link, notes) are optional. Added companies are saved permanently in your browser and support all filters, searching, status tracking, and sorting just like built-in companies. To remove a custom company, expand its row in the table and click "Remove company".
↺ Reset All
The Reset All button (top right, in red) clears everything you've personalised: all application statuses, custom companies you've added, and any field edits (dates, intake, type, etc.): returning the tracker to its original state. A confirmation prompt will appear before anything is deleted, so you won't do it by accident.
⊞ Table vs Card View
Use the ☰ Table view for a dense, sortable overview of all companies at once: best for comparing and updating statuses quickly. Use the ⊞ Card view for a more visual browsing experience, where each company's key details are laid out at a glance. All filters and search work identically in both views.
Tip: Click any row in Table view to expand it and see full notes, the complete careers link, and the remove button for custom companies.
ℹ️ A Note on the Data
Application dates, intake sizes, and student interest levels are estimates based on historical patterns: they are not official figures from the companies. Always check each company's careers page directly for the most current openings and deadlines. Dates in particular can shift from year to year.

Hi, I'm Rohith, an actuarial student based in Australia.

As a first year, I found it confusing and difficult to keep track of early opportunities. Everything seemed to be aimed at later years, and there was no clear picture of what was actually out there.

This only continued when I started applying for internship and graduate opportunities. I quickly realised there was no good tool built for this. Generic job trackers don't understand the actuarial market: they don't know the difference between a pricing role and a reserving role, they don't track pre-penultimate programmes, the links aren't easy to access, and they certainly don't give you the tips and tricks available here.

So I built one. I spent time researching every major actuarial employer in Australia across every sector: general insurance, reinsurance, life, health, superannuation, banking, consulting, investments, and government, and compiled everything into a single, well-designed tracker that I actually wanted to use.

Once it was working, it felt wrong to keep it to myself. Every actuarial student going through applications faces the same problem, so I made it free and shared it with anyone who'd find it useful.

I update it regularly based on user feedback. If you spot something wrong, out of date, or think something's missing: hit the Feedback button. I read every submission.

Good luck with your applications. The market is competitive but there's more than enough room for well-prepared candidates, and now you have one less thing to worry about.
